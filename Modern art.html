Modern Art

Modern Art was mainly produced from the 1860s to the 1970s.
It was created to challenge the art and traditions that came before it.
Some would call modern art a 'rejection of history'.
Some of the famous modern art techniques included dripping, pouring, and scraping.

Examples of Modern Art

Modern Art walks the line between simplistic modernism and over the top chaos.

Modern Artists

Some of the more popular modern artists
include Jeff Koons, Yayoi Kusama, and
Damien Hurst. These artists are all
famous for going outside the expectations
of society and creating something unique yet modern.

History of Modern Art

As MoMA Learning explains, Modern Art can be traced all
the way back to the Industrial Revolution! Art used to be
created for the wealthy, of the wealthy;
however, going more into the 19th century and thanks to
Sigmund Freud's, 'The Interpretation of Dreams', artists
began creating art exploring their own subconcious experiences.
This was done using many different mediums, techniques, and materials.

History of Modern Art by MoMa

Modern Museums

There are many incredible museums of modern art,
including the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art,
the Modern Art Museum of Forth
Worth, and the Museum of Contemporary Art
in Los Angeles. However, the most
famous Museum of Modern Art is the MoMA.
Located in New York City. MoMA intertwines
all types of modern art from different
mediums and technologies.
